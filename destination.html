Summer Getaways

"One must maintain a little bit of summer, even in the middle of winter."-Henry David Thoreau.

Locals and tourists in a tropical paradise like the Philippines like to cool off by having the waves on their backs and sand on their toes, especially during the summer vacation. Aside from going to white-sand beaches to surf, and snorkel, nature lovers go on mountain climbing and biking during the summer season.

Pink Beach

santa cruz island

Great Santa Cruz Island is a small inhabited island in Zamboanga City in the southern region of the Philippines that is famous for its pink coralline sand.

Merloquet

Merloquet Falls

It is a two-tiered drop but its main attraction is the beautiful curtain waterfalls on its base, approximately 10-meter high and 15-meter wide stair-like wall to a shallow pool, definitely one-of-a-kind in the Philippines.

Paseo

Paseo Del Mar

Paseo Del Mar is located very close to the historical landmark and National Museum known as Fort Pilar. Paseo Del Mar is also known for its several restaurants, cafés and entertainment as well.

Old Tales

"A generation which ignores history has no past and no future."-Robert Heinlein

The Zamboanga Peninsula has a wealth of historical landmarks that offer a glimpse into the province's understated historical significance.

Zamboanga City Hall

Filipino colonial design

The building served as the official residence of the US military governor of the Moro Province. It later became the seat of government of the Department of Mindanao and Sulu and the defunct Zamboanga province.

Fort Pilar Shrine

fortaleza del pilar

Also called Fortaleza del Pilar, Fort Pilar dates back to the 17th century, originally serving as a Spanish military defense post. Fast forward to the present, this bastioned fort is a major historical landmark of the city.

Culture and Tradition

“Traditions are the guideposts driven deep in our subconscious minds. The most powerful ones are those we can't even describe, aren't even aware of” -Ellen Goodman

Chavacano, Zamboanga City's principal culture, evolved from the Subanen with Chinese, Muslim, Spanish, Japanese, American, and other ethnic cultures. The Tausugs from the island province of Sulu, on the other hand, give Zamboanga a touch of Moro and Arab, especially in its cuisine."

Yakan

Yakan Weaving

The Yakan weaving is known for the intricacy of its. designs, the eye-catching and colorful textiles with tiny motifs used in the woven clothes and garbs.

Curacha

Curacha in Alavar Sauce

Curacha Alavar, sometimes referred to as curacha con salsa Alavar ("Curacha with Alavar sauce") in Chavacano a Spanish-based creole language, is a Filipino dish made from spanner crabs (curacha), garlic, ginger, salt, and Alavar sauce.

Colorful Vinta

Regatta de Zamboanga

The vinta is a traditional boat that has sails with assorted vertical colors that represent the colorful culture and history of the Muslim community.Normally, you can only see them set sail during the Regatta festival held every October.
